Basavanagudi-Specific Vastu Insight

Basavanagudi's Vastu is defined by its sacred geography: the Dodda Ganesha temple and the Nandi Bull temple sit on Bugle Hill's South-West ridge — a position that in Vastu corresponds to the Earth element stability zone. This placement amplifies the South-West energy across the entire locality, creating an unusually strong stability field that promotes wealth preservation and social standing. However, properties directly in the temple's shadow (those to the North and East of the temples) receive the deity's energy from the South-West, which creates a directional dominance in the stability zone at the expense of the North-East prosperity energy. Properties to the South and West of the temples, conversely, receive the deity's forward-facing energy toward their North-East — an exceptionally auspicious configuration. A VIDS™ assessment for Basavanagudi properties always analyses the exact temple-to-property directional relationship.
