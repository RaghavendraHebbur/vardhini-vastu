Post-renovation Vastu reassessment is strongly recommended. Renovations frequently alter the energy of zones — an extended kitchen may now occupy the East zone instead of the South-East, a new bathroom may have been added in the North-East, or internal walls may have shifted the Brahmasthan boundary. Even well-intentioned renovations can inadvertently create Vastu defects. A post-renovation VIDS™ assessment identifies any new issues and provides immediate zero-demolition corrections.

South-facing homes are among the most misunderstood in Vastu — they are not inherently inauspicious, but the specific pada (subsection) of the South wall where the entrance falls makes a significant difference. Certain South-facing padas are acceptable while others create challenges. Additionally, a South-facing home requires the North Kuber (wealth) zone to be actively energised with water features and wealth yantras to compensate for the financial energy that the South entrance orientation may suppress. A consultation assesses your specific entrance pada and prescribes the complete correction set.
