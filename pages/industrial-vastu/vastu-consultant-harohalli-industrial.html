Industrial Vastu FAQ — Harohalli Industrial Area

Vastu's directional principles naturally align with industrial safety requirements. The fire element zones (South-East) are appropriate for heat-generating and reactive chemical processes. The water element zones (North-East) support water-based processes and cooling systems. Heavy hazardous material storage belongs in the South-West Earth zone — which in safety planning is also where heavy, stable, locked storage belongs. Vastu's zone allocation and industrial safety zoning frequently recommend the same spatial arrangements for very different reasons — creating a powerful combined argument for correct zone usage.
The expansion of residential development along the Kanakapura Road corridor creates a mixed-use energy environment where residential Vayu and water elements blend with industrial Earth and fire elements. For Harohalli industrial units near the residential-industrial boundary, this mixing can create zone confusion unless the factory's internal zone boundaries are clearly defined and maintained through specific remedies. A clear compound wall with appropriate height in the South and South-West helps maintain the factory's industrial energy distinctness from adjacent residential zones.
Borewell and overhead tank positions are among the most impactful Vastu elements for industrial premises. The borewell (underground water source) should be in the North-East zone — most auspicious for water energy. The overhead tank (which adds weight and water element simultaneously) should be in the South-West zone — where weight is beneficial and where the water above provides a counterbalancing element to the Earth below. If your borewell is in the South-East or South-West, or your overhead tank is in the North-East or North, specific corrections are required.
