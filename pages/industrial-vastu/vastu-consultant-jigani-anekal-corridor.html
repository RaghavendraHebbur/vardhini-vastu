Industrial Vastu FAQ — Jigani Anekal Corridor

A comparative Vastu assessment of plots along the corridor evaluates: gate direction (North or East preferred), any irregular shape or corner cuts, proximity to T-junctions or road dead-ends, underground water table depth and direction (North-East is auspicious for water sources), overhead power line direction, and the plot's elevation relative to adjacent land (ideally higher in South-West and lower in North-East). We can conduct a pre-purchase multi-plot assessment in a single visit and provide a comparative ranking with recommendations.
Cold storage and food processing have specific Vastu requirements: cold storage belongs in the South or South-West zones (Earth element — stability and preservation), while active food processing involving heat belongs in the South-East (fire zone). The corridor's relatively open landscape and good road access make it suitable for these functions. The key Vastu check for food industry units is water source direction (North-East is auspicious for water-intensive food processing) and waste disposal direction (South or North-West, away from the main production area).
Multi-gate logistics hubs require a Vastu analysis of each gate's directional energy and its role in the operational flow. The main entry gate should face North or East. The truck entry gate for goods-in can face North or East as well. The exit gate for goods-out can face West or South. Separate vehicle and personnel entry points should be zoned so that the personnel main entrance is in the most auspicious position. We map all gate positions and recommend zone-appropriate operational assignments.
