Vastu for Mirrors: Placement, Direction & Rules

The golden rule of mirror vastu — what a mirror reflects determines whether it amplifies prosperity or creates defects in your home.

Mirrors are one of the most energetically powerful objects in any home — because they reflect, double, and redirect whatever energy they face. This doubling property makes mirrors one of the most useful vastu remedies when correctly placed, and one of the most common sources of vastu defects when incorrectly placed. Understanding what a mirror reflects — and whether doubling that energy is beneficial or harmful — is the core principle of mirror vastu.

The Golden Rule: What Is the Mirror Reflecting?

Before placing any mirror, ask: what will this mirror reflect? If doubling that energy is beneficial — place it. If doubling it is harmful — move it.

  • Reflects open space, light, or a beautiful view: Doubles positive, expansive energy — auspicious
  • Reflects the dining table with food: Doubles abundance — auspicious
  • Reflects a water feature or aquarium: Doubles Water element prosperity energy — auspicious
  • Reflects a sleeping body: Doubles fatigue and vulnerability — inauspicious
  • Reflects the main door directly: Reflects incoming positive energy back out — inauspicious
  • Reflects clutter or disorder: Doubles the negative, stagnant energy of clutter — remove clutter before placing mirrors
  • Reflects a toilet or bathroom: Doubles waste energy — remove the mirror or reposition it
  • Reflects a sharp corner, beam, or pillar: Doubles the cutting energy of architectural defects — do not use a mirror to "hide" a sharp corner

Specific Mirror Rules

Main Entrance Mirror

A mirror directly facing the main door reflects all incoming positive energy back outside. This is one of the most common vastu defects in modern homes where large wall mirrors are placed in entrance foyers.

Remedy: Angle the mirror so it does not face the door directly, or replace with a mirror on the side wall of the entrance hall.

Bedroom Mirror

The most frequently violated mirror rule. The bedroom mirror must not reflect the bed. This includes mirrors on wardrobes opposite the bed, dressing mirrors facing the bed, and decorative mirrors on the wall that the bed faces.

Remedy: Cover at night with a cloth, relocate, or replace mirrored wardrobe doors with non-reflective alternatives.

Dining Room Mirror

The one room where a mirror is actively recommended for its reflective property. A mirror on the North or East wall reflecting the dining table doubles the food and abundance — one of the most auspicious mirror placements in all of vastu.

Mirror Size, Shape and Condition

  • Full-length mirrors: Best placed in dressing areas or bathrooms — should not face sleeping areas
  • Large wall mirrors in living areas: Excellent on the North wall — expand perceived space and double prosperity energy
  • Round mirrors: Softest energy — good for living areas and hallways
  • Square/rectangular mirrors: Classic, stable energy — appropriate for most rooms
  • Irregular or jagged-edged mirrors: Irregular reflections create irregular energy — avoid as primary room mirrors
  • Cracked or damaged mirrors: Remove immediately — a cracked mirror reflects fragmented energy and is one of the clearest vastu defect signals in the home
