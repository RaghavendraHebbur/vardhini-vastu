7 Vastu Rules for the Bedroom

Rule 1: Master Bedroom in the South-West Zone

Method: The master bedroom should occupy the South-West zone of the home. This is the heaviest, most stable zone in the Vastu grid, governed by the Prithvi (Earth) element. The SW's weight and stability directly support the head of household's authority, longevity, decision-making capacity, and financial consistency. Children or guests sleeping in the SW will unconsciously dominate the household energy, which is a common but overlooked cause of parent-child authority conflicts and family hierarchy imbalances.

Rule 2: Head Points South or East While Sleeping

Method: Position the bed so the sleeper's head points South or East. South is the primary recommendation because the human body functions like a magnetic dipole — with the head as the north-seeking pole. When the head points South, it aligns harmoniously with Earth's magnetic south, reducing electromagnetic stress on the brain and enabling deeper, more restorative sleep cycles. East is acceptable and brings solar vitality and mental freshness upon waking. North is strictly contraindicated: head-North creates magnetic repulsion, and ancient texts specifically associate it with illness and shortened lifespan.

Rule 3: Place the Bed in the SW Corner of the Room

Method: Within the bedroom itself, position the bed in the South-West corner of the room. Leave a small gap — even 2 to 3 inches — between the bed and both the South and West walls. This gap allows prana to circulate around the entire sleeping body rather than becoming stagnant against solid walls. The bed should never float in the centre of the room (lack of back support creates subconscious insecurity) and never touch the North or East walls (blocks incoming energy in the receiving zones).

Rule 4: Never Place a Mirror Directly Facing the Bed

Method: A mirror that reflects the sleeping form is one of the most serious Vastu defects in a bedroom. According to classical Vastu and corroborating feng shui principles, the sleeping body is in its most vulnerable, yin state — and a mirror doubles the energetic signature of that vulnerability, creating chronic sleep disturbances, vivid disturbing dreams, and progressive health drain. If an existing wardrobe mirror or dressing mirror faces the bed, cover it with a light cloth at night. Relocating the mirror to the North or East wall (where it does not reflect the bed) is the permanent solution.

Rule 5: No TV in the Bedroom — If Unavoidable, Cover the Screen

Method: A television in the bedroom introduces the Fire element (electronics, EMF) into a room governed by Earth and yin rest energy. Beyond the Vastu elemental conflict, a switched-off TV screen acts as a low-grade mirror, reflecting the sleeping form at night and causing the same energy drain as a mirror defect. If a TV cannot be removed, mount it on the South-East wall (Fire element in the correct Agni zone) and drape a cloth over the screen every night before sleep. This simple act eliminates the mirror-reflection defect entirely.

Rule 6: No Water Features or Fish Tanks in the Bedroom

Method: Water features, aquariums, and even large vases of water activate the Jal (Water) element, which is an active, yang, flowing energy. The bedroom is a yin rest zone where the goal is stillness, restoration, and withdrawal from activity. An active Water element in the bedroom creates energetic turbulence that manifests as restlessness, fragmented sleep, excessive thinking at night, and in couples, as recurring emotional conflicts around finances and security. Remove all water features from the bedroom and relocate them to the North (Kuber) or North-East (Isha) zones of the living area.

Rule 7: Ensure the Bedroom Door Opens to a Full 90 Degrees

Method: The bedroom door is the primary energy intake point for the room. A door that can only open to 45° or 60° because furniture is placed behind it restricts the room's full energetic intake — similar to breathing through a partially blocked airway. Ensure the door swings to a full 90° with no obstruction behind it. If the door opens to reveal a direct view of the bed (bed visible immediately upon opening the door), this is a minor Vastu defect called "coffin position" in feng shui — remedy it by angling the bed so it is not the first thing visible from the doorway.

Classical Vastu texts including the Brihat Samhita and the Vastu Vidya of Kerala devote entire chapters to the sleeping chamber (shayan griha), specifying not only direction and zone but the optimal head-to-door relationship, the correct wood for the bed frame (teak and sheesham are recommended; iron beds are cautioned against), and the ideal wall colours for each zone. The South-West bedroom rule is consistent across all regional schools of Vastu — Tamil, Malayalam, Kannada, and North Indian — making it one of the most universally agreed principles in the entire science.
