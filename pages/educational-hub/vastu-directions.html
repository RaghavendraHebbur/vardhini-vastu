Vastu Shastra Directions: All 8 Directions, Deities & Meanings (2026)

Direction is the primary language of Vastu Shastra. Before a single room is placed, before a single wall is constructed, the orientation of a space in relation to the eight compass directions determines the energy field it will inhabit. Each direction is not merely a point on a compass — in vastu, each direction is a living field of cosmic energy, governed by a specific deity, planet, and element that actively influences the inhabitants of any space facing or positioned within it.

The 8 Directions: Complete Reference

North — Uttara (उत्तर)

DeityKuber — Lord of Wealth
PlanetMercury (Budha)
ElementWater (secondary)
ColourGreen, sea-green, light blue
Best roomsLiving room, home office, study, treasury/safe
GovernsWealth, financial abundance, career growth, new opportunities

North is the direction of Kuber — the regent of wealth and the guardian of all earthly treasures. Every home's north zone is its financial energy field. A clear, open, well-lit north zone supports career advancement and steady financial growth. The safe or locker should open towards north — directly facing Kuber. Blocked north zones (heavy storage, dark walls, large furniture) suppress financial energy and career opportunities.

North-East — Ishaan (ईशान)

DeityIshaan (Shiva) — Lord of All Directions
PlanetJupiter (Brihaspati/Guru)
ElementWater
ColourWhite, sea-green, light yellow, crystal
Best roomsPooja room, meditation space, water feature, study
GovernsSpirituality, wisdom, divine grace, education, comprehensive prosperity

The north-east is the most sacred and energetically powerful zone in any floor plan. Governed by Ishaan (a form of Shiva, lord of all directions) and Jupiter (planet of wisdom and expansion), this zone receives the combined wealth energy of the north and the life energy of the east, filtered through Jupiter's divine intelligence. It must always be the lightest, most open, most sacred zone of the home — kept clean, illuminated, and honoured. Any defect in the NE corner (toilet, kitchen, heavy storage, staircase) causes the most severe vastu problems.

East — Purva (पूर्व)

DeityIndra — King of the Gods
PlanetSun (Surya)
ElementFire (solar)
ColourWhite, light yellow, cream, gold
Best roomsLiving room, main entrance, children's room, bathroom (attached)
GovernsHealth, vitality, social prestige, authority, fame

East is the direction of sunrise — the source of light, life, and vitality on earth. Indra, the king of the gods, governs the east — giving east-facing spaces a natural quality of authority and social prestige. Morning sunlight entering from the east carries the most beneficial spectrum of solar energy: it activates vitamin D synthesis, regulates circadian rhythms, boosts immunity, and elevates mood. East walls and east-facing rooms should have maximum windows and openings. East-facing main doors are among the most auspicious in vastu.

South-East — Agneya (आग्नेय)

DeityAgni — God of Fire
PlanetVenus (Shukra)
ElementFire
ColourRed, orange, bright yellow, gold
Best roomsKitchen, generator room, electrical room, server room
GovernsEnergy, transformation, creativity, passion, technical skill

The south-east is the Fire zone — both literally and cosmically. The kitchen belongs here because Agni governs both the cosmic fire energy of this direction and the physical fire used in cooking. Venus (Shukra) as co-ruler adds creativity, beauty, and passion to this zone. All electrical equipment, server rooms, and generators belong in the SE because they all work with controlled fire energy. An overactive SE (fireplace, multiple heat sources) creates excess fire — tempers, accidents, and financial burning.

South — Dakshina (दक्षिण)

DeityYama — Lord of Dharma and Justice
PlanetMars (Mangal)
ElementEarth (secondary)
ColourYellow, orange, earthy tones
Best roomsSecondary bedrooms, storage, staircase
GovernsDharma, justice, discipline, long-term wealth, authority

Yama governs the south — and this is widely misunderstood. Yama is not the god of death in the colloquial sense — he is the lord of Dharma (righteous living) and the protector of cosmic justice. South-facing homes governed by Yama are excellent for business, law, government, and any profession that requires authority and discipline. The south zone within a home is good for secondary bedrooms and storage — heavier, denser activities that are less interactive. The compound wall on the south side should be the tallest.

South-West — Nairuti (नैऋत्य)

DeityNairuti — Deity of Dissolution
PlanetRahu (shadow planet)
ElementEarth
ColourBrown, terracotta, dark yellow, dark orange
Best roomsMaster bedroom, heavy storage, safe, treasury
GovernsEarth energy, stability, dissolution, transformation, accumulated wealth

The south-west is the heaviest, most stable, and most protective zone in any floor plan. Nairuti governs dissolution — endings, completions, and the fullness of accumulated experience. The master bedroom belongs here because this Earth energy grounds the head of the family, provides authority and stability, and ensures deep, restorative sleep. The SW should always be the zone with the most physical weight: heaviest furniture, highest walls, densest construction. A light or deficient SW zone destabilises the entire household.

West — Paschima (पश्चिम)

DeityVarun — Lord of Cosmic Order and Waters
PlanetSaturn (Shani)
ElementWater (secondary)
ColourBlue, grey, dark blue, silver
Best roomsChildren's room, study, dining room, toilet (secondary)
GovernsCosmic order, discipline, sustained effort, long-term success

Varun governs the west — the direction of the setting sun and the cosmic ocean. Saturn, the planet of discipline, longevity, and sustained effort, rules this zone. West-zone rooms are excellent for study (Saturn rewards discipline with results), children's rooms (discipline and focus), and dining rooms (Saturn governs sustenance through effort). West-facing homes receive afternoon sun — manageable with a deep verandah. Saturn's energy in the west rewards those who work consistently, making west-zone home offices excellent for long-term projects.

North-West — Vayavya (वायव्य)

DeityVayu — God of Wind
PlanetMoon (Chandra)
ElementAir
ColourWhite, silver, light grey, cream
Best roomsGuest room, toilet, children's room, garage
GovernsAir, movement, change, social connections, transience

Vayu and the Moon govern the north-west — creating a zone of movement, change, and social dynamism. Guests belong here because Vayu energy keeps them mobile (they naturally feel the urge to conclude their visit). Vehicles belong here because they are inherently mobile. The Moon's influence makes this zone emotionally fluid and socially intuitive — excellent for bedrooms of children who need to develop social skills, or for social media and communication professionals.

The 16-Zone VIDS™ System

Standard vastu uses these 8 directions. The VIDS™ (Vardhini Integrated Direction System) divides each direction into 2 sub-zones, creating 16 zones of 22.5° each — NNE, NE, ENE, E, ESE, SE, SSE, S, SSW, SW, WSW, W, WNW, NW, NNW, N. This doubles analytical precision: the difference between a door in the NNE sub-zone versus the NE sub-zone is significant, and standard 8-zone analysis cannot distinguish them.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the 8 directions in vastu shastra?

North (Kuber/Mercury), NE (Ishaan/Jupiter), East (Indra/Sun), SE (Agni/Venus), South (Yama/Mars), SW (Nairuti/Rahu), West (Varun/Saturn), NW (Vayu/Moon).

Which direction is best for a house in vastu?

Any direction can be auspicious with correct configuration. North and East are generally most recommended; South and West require specific main door pada placement; SW is the most challenging but manageable with professional corrections.

Which direction should I sleep in vastu?

Head pointing South (best) — aligns with Earth's magnetic field. Head pointing East (second best) — aligns with sunrise energy. Never head pointing North — magnetic disruption disturbs sleep.
