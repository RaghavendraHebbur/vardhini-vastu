Room-by-Room Vastu for West Facing House

Master Bedroom

Place the master bedroom in the south-west corner — the same rule applies to all house orientations. The south-west is Earth zone, providing stability, sound sleep, and authority. Head should point south or east.

Kitchen

South-east is ideal in a west-facing house too. The cook faces east while cooking. If south-east is constrained by layout, the north-west is an acceptable secondary position (with the cook facing east or north).

Pooja Room

North-east corner — the Ishaan zone. Even though it is at the back of the house in a west-facing layout, the north-east remains the most sacred and correct position for the pooja room. Keep this area well-lit with natural or artificial light since it is deeper in the home.

Children's Room

West or north-west zones. In a west-facing house, the children's rooms can be positioned closer to the front (west side) in a secondary bedroom configuration, using the 3rd or 4th zone of the west wall (away from the main entrance area).

Guest Room

North-west is ideal. Vayu energy supports the transient nature of guests. In a west-facing home, the north-west is naturally positioned well for guest access without disrupting private family zones.

Living Room

North or north-east zones. Keep the living room light and open, with windows facing north or east. Use the west-facing entrance as a transitional space (lobby or foyer) rather than jumping directly into the living room.

Bathroom / Toilet

Acceptable: west (away from entrance), north-west, south. Avoid north-east absolutely. Avoid placing a toilet immediately adjacent to the pooja room wall.

Study Room / Home Office

North zone (Kuber) is best. In a west-facing house, the north zone is at the side of the home — create a quiet study away from the west-facing entrance noise and afternoon light.

Staircase

South, south-west, or west zones (away from the main entrance area). Rise from north to south or east to west.

Common Vastu Defects in West Facing Houses and Remedies

Defect 1: Afternoon Sun Overheating the Entrance

A practical challenge of west-facing homes is afternoon heat entering through the main door. Remedy: Build a deep west-facing verandah or porch (minimum 4–6 feet deep). Use thick curtains or a jali screen on the west entrance. This is a structural comfort measure that also improves vastu energy management by creating a transitional zone.

Defect 2: Main Door in 8th or 9th Pada

Remedy: Virtual Gate Opening technique to shift the energetic entry to the 5th pada (Varun). Copper Varun yantra installed at the threshold. Paint the area around the existing door in blue or silver tones to invoke Varun energy even from an incorrect pada.

Defect 3: North-East Zone Used as Storage or Utility

Common in west-facing homes where north-east is the deepest interior zone. Remedy: Clear all clutter. Install a small crystal water feature. Keep a lamp burning in the evening. Use light yellow or white in this zone.

Defect 4: Heavy South-West Boundary Wall Lower Than North

Remedy: In a west-facing house, ensure the south boundary wall (behind the house) is higher or equal to the north boundary wall. The south-west must always be the heaviest structural zone.

West Facing House: Do's and Don'ts

Do's

  • Place the main door precisely in the 3rd, 4th, or 5th pada of the west wall
  • Build a deep verandah on the west side to manage afternoon heat
  • Keep the north-east open, light, and sacred — even though it is at the back
  • Use blue or silver accents on the west-facing exterior (Varun colours)
  • Keep south-west heavier than north-east
  • Open north and east windows for morning light and Kuber/Indra energy

Don'ts

  • Do not place the main door in the 1st, 8th, or 9th pada
  • Do not block the north-east zone with storage or heavy furniture
  • Do not place a toilet or kitchen in the north-east
  • Do not use red or fiery colours on the west exterior (conflicts with Varun energy)
  • Do not place large trees or compound wall extensions blocking the north or east
