The Truth About South Facing Houses in Vastu

South is governed by Yama — the deity of dharma, justice, and righteousness. This does not mean death or misfortune; it means law, authority, and long-term stability. South-facing homes are particularly well-suited for:

  • Business owners and entrepreneurs (stability, long-term wealth)
  • Lawyers, judges, and government officials (authority, justice)
  • Politicians (public authority)
  • Anyone in a position of leadership or administration

The real problem is not the south facing direction — it is the main door placed in the wrong pada. The south wall has only one or two acceptable positions for the main entrance. When the door lands anywhere else, the negative effects are real and measurable. This is where most south-facing houses go wrong — and why the direction got its bad reputation.

Room-by-Room Vastu for South Facing House

Master Bedroom

The south-west corner is the ideal master bedroom in a south-facing house — the same as in all orientations. The south-west is the zone of Earth element and heaviness, providing stability and groundedness for the head of the family. Sleep with head pointing south.

Kitchen

The south-east corner is ideal. In a south-facing house, the south-east zone is adjacent to the south entrance area, so careful planning is needed to ensure kitchen and entrance zones don't overlap. The cook should face east. If south-east is unavailable, north-west is an acceptable alternative.

Pooja Room

Place the pooja room in the north-east corner. In a south-facing house, the north-east is at the back of the home — this is still the most sacred zone and the correct location for prayer and meditation. The idol faces west or east.

Living Room

The north or north-east zones work well. In a south-facing house, these zones are deeper inside the home (away from the entrance), which creates a private, calm living space. Keep these zones well-lit and clutter-free.

Children's Room

West or north-west zones are ideal. Children sleeping in the west zone benefit from discipline, focus, and academic consistency.

Guest Room

North-west is ideal for the guest room. In a south-facing house, this position also keeps guests away from the north-east (sacred zone) and the south-west (master bedroom).

Bathroom / Toilet

Acceptable zones: south (but not blocking the main door), west, north-west. Avoid north-east absolutely. Avoid south-west (this weakens the master bedroom zone).

Staircase

In a south-facing house, the staircase works well in the south (western side of south), south-west, or west. Avoid north-east. The stairs should rise from north to south.

South Facing House: Key Do's and Don'ts

Do's

  • Ensure the main door is precisely in the 4th pada of the south wall
  • Keep south and south-west zones heavier than north and north-east
  • Use earth tones (terracotta, brown, dark yellow) in the south and south-west
  • Keep the north-east zone open, clean, and sacred
  • Install a higher compound wall on the north side than the south
  • Use a Vastu compass or VIDS™ degree measurement to verify all placements

Don'ts

  • Do not place the main door in any pada other than the 4th (or 3rd as fallback)
  • Do not make the north-east the heaviest zone in the house
  • Do not place a toilet or kitchen in the north-east
  • Do not use light colours like white or pale yellow on the south-facing exterior wall
  • Do not assume south-facing is bad without verifying the door pada first
