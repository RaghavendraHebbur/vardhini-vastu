Why North Facing Houses Are Considered Auspicious

In Vastu Shastra, the north direction is associated with three powerful forces:

  • Kuber (Lord of Wealth) — governs financial inflow and accumulation
  • Soma (Moon energy) — brings mental clarity, calm, and good relationships
  • Magnetic north — aligns the earth's natural magnetic field with the home's primary axis

When the main entrance faces north, positive cosmic energy — known as pranic energy — enters the home through the most energetically active face. The result is a house that naturally supports the residents in building wealth, maintaining health, and nurturing family harmony.

However, this benefit is conditional. A north facing house with the main door in the wrong pada (position), a kitchen in the wrong zone, or a blocked north-east corner will neutralise — and even reverse — these benefits.

Room-by-Room Vastu for North Facing House

Master Bedroom

Place the master bedroom in the south-west corner. This is the zone of Earth element and Pitru energy — it provides stability, groundedness, and deep sleep. The head of the family sleeping here gains authority and strong decision-making ability. The bed should be positioned so the head points south or east during sleep.

Avoid: North-east master bedroom — this zone is for meditation and prayer, not sleep. Sleeping here causes restlessness and weakened health over time.

Kitchen

The ideal kitchen position in a north facing house is the south-east corner — the zone of Agni (fire element). The cook should face east while cooking. This alignment activates the fire element correctly and supports good health for all family members.

Avoid: North-east kitchen. This is one of the most serious vastu defects — it suppresses wealth, causes digestive issues, and creates conflict between family members.

Pooja Room / Mandir

Place the pooja room in the north-east corner. This zone — the Ishaan corner — is the most sacred in any home. Governed by the Water element and Jupiter's energy, it amplifies prayers and spiritual practices. The idol should face west (so the worshipper faces east) or face east (worshipper faces west).

Children's Room

The west or north-west zone is ideal for children's rooms. West is governed by Varun (water deity) and supports study, discipline, and academic achievement. Children sleeping with their head pointing east improve focus and memory.

Guest Room

Place the guest room in the north-west zone. This is the Vayu (air) zone — energy here is transient and mobile, making it perfect for short-term occupants like guests. Guests who stay in north-west rooms naturally feel the urge to leave on time.

Living Room / Drawing Room

The north or north-east zones are excellent for the living room in a north facing house. Heavy furniture (sofas, cabinets) should be placed in the south or west of the living room. Keep the north and east sides lighter and more open.

Bathroom / Toilet

Toilets should be in the south, south-east (if kitchen is not there), north-west, or west zones. Never place a toilet in the north-east — this is the most damaging placement in any house and causes severe financial and health problems.

Study Room

The west zone or the north zone are ideal for a study room. Students should face east or north while studying. A study room in the north benefits from Kuber's energy, supporting focus on financial studies, commerce, and careers.

Staircase

In a north facing house, the staircase should be in the south, south-west, or west zone. Staircases in the north-east are a serious defect — they block incoming positive energy. Stairs should always rise from north to south or from east to west (i.e., you walk up towards south or west).

Common Vastu Defects in North Facing Houses (and Remedies)

Defect 1: Main Door in Wrong Pada

Remedy: A Virtual Gate Opening technique — one of the core VIDS™ correction methods — can energetically shift the entry point without any physical demolition. A copper threshold plate with specific yantric inscription at the existing doorstep also neutralises the effect of a wrongly placed door.

Defect 2: Kitchen in North-East

Remedy: Activate the south-east zone with a pyramid grid and relocate cooking activity (even a temporary stove) to the south-east. Use a Fire Activation Strip along the south-east wall. This is a documented zero-demolition correction used in the VIDS™ system.

Defect 3: Blocked or Extended North Zone

Remedy: Ensure the north side of the house has the least obstruction. Remove heavy furniture, dark curtains, or clutter from the north wall. Paint the north wall in sea-green or light blue. Place a small water feature (bowl of water with floating flower) in the north zone.

Defect 4: Toilet in North-East

Remedy: This is one of the most serious defects. Until structural renovation is possible, place a Vastu pyramid grid under the toilet floor and use a sea-green door cover. Place Himalayan salt in a ceramic bowl in the north-east bathroom to absorb negative energy.

Defect 5: South-West Not Elevated / Heavy

Remedy: Add weight to the south-west — install heavier furniture, use dark earth tones (brown, terracotta) in décor. Ensure the south-west room has the highest ceiling thickness or structural beam if possible.

North Facing House Vastu: Key Do's and Don'ts

Do's

  • Keep the north and north-east zones open, light, and clutter-free
  • Place a water body (aquarium, fountain, water bowl) in the north or north-east
  • Use light, cool colors (white, light blue, sea-green) in north-facing rooms
  • Ensure the north-east corner is the most sacred and clean spot in the house
  • Keep the south-west zone heavier than the north-east
  • Plant money plants or holy basil (tulsi) in the north or north-east

Don'ts

  • Do not place a toilet, kitchen, or staircase in the north-east
  • Do not build a boundary wall higher on the north side than the south
  • Do not place heavy machinery or generators in the north-east
  • Do not park vehicles in the north-east zone
  • Do not use dark, heavy colors on the north wall
  • Do not install a septic tank in the north or north-east
