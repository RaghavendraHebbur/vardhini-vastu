Vastu Yantras: 7 Essential Yantras and Their Placement Rules

Kuber yantra for the North, Saraswati yantra for the study, Vastu Purush yantra for the whole home — the complete guide to vastu yantras, their zones, and their activations.

Vastu yantras are the most precise and traditionally authoritative correction tools in the vastu system — sacred geometric instruments that create specific, sustained energy fields in the zones where they are placed. Unlike more general tools like crystals or pyramids, each vastu yantra is dedicated to a specific deity, zone energy, or life domain — making them highly targeted activators for the exact goals they are designed to serve. A Kuber yantra placed on the North wall is not a general prosperity tool — it is specifically the energetic presence of Kuber himself, installed in his own zone, activating his specific domain of financial and career prosperity.

Vastu yantras are traditionally made of copper (the primary prana conductor in Indian metallurgy), brass, or gold-plated copper. The yantra must be energetically activated — either through a consecration ritual (prana pratishtha) performed by a qualified priest, or through a personal activation ritual of cleaning, intention-setting, and mantra recitation. An unactivated yantra is a geometric diagram; an activated yantra is a living energy device.

7 Essential Vastu Yantras and Their Placements

Yantra 1: Vastu Purush Yantra — Whole-Home Harmonisation

Method: Place the Vastu Purush Yantra either under the floor at the Brahmasthana (centre of the home) during construction, or on the wall at the main entrance threshold for existing properties. The Vastu Purush Yantra activates all 16 zones of the home simultaneously by establishing the correct energy of Vastu Purush — the cosmic being whose body is the home — in the property's energy field. This is the most comprehensive single vastu yantra correction available and is recommended as the foundational installation in any property undergoing a full vastu correction.

Yantra 2: Kuber Yantra — Prosperity and Career

Method: Place the copper Kuber Yantra on the North wall of the living room or home office at eye level, facing South (worshipper faces North). Offer fresh flowers, a lit diya, and a short prayer on Thursdays — Kuber's day. The Kuber yantra is the most widely used of all vastu yantras for financial goals — it directly activates the North zone's prosperity channel, attracts incoming career opportunities, and establishes Kuber's divine presence in the home's financial energy zone.

Yantra 3: Sri Yantra — Divine Abundance

Method: Place the Sri Yantra (Shri Yantra) in the NE zone of the home, in the puja room, or on the East wall facing West (worshipper faces East). The Sri Yantra is the geometric form of Goddess Lakshmi — the most sacred and powerful of all vastu yantras for abundance and prosperity. Its 9-triangle interlocking geometry represents the union of cosmic male and female energies, creating a field of divine abundance that transcends any single life domain. The Sri Yantra in the NE puja room is the most auspicious and comprehensive prosperity installation in the vastu yantras tradition.

Yantra 4: Saraswati Yantra — Knowledge and Learning

Method: Place the Saraswati Yantra on the East wall of the study room or children's study area — at eye level when seated at the desk, facing West (student faces East toward the yantra). Activate on Vasant Panchami (Saraswati Puja day) for maximum potency. The Saraswati yantra is the primary vastu yantra for educational and academic goals — it directly activates the learning energy of the East zone and establishes Saraswati's presence as the presiding deity of the study space.

Yantra 5: Nairuti Yantra — Protection and Relationships

Method: Place the Nairuti Yantra on the South-West wall of the home — inside the SW room or on the outer SW wall if the SW has a defect (missing corner, water feature, or heavy activity zone). The Nairuti yantra strengthens the SW zone's Earth element boundary and protection energy — correcting relationship instability, financial retention defects, and the authority zone weakness that manifests as difficulty in maintaining boundaries in personal and professional life.

Yantra 6: Vastu Dosh Nivaran Yantra — Defect Neutralisation

Method: Install the Vastu Dosh Nivaran Yantra under the main entrance door mat or fix it to the back of the main door. This yantra is specifically designed to neutralise structural vastu defects that cannot be physically corrected — NE toilet, SW entrance, missing corners, and T-junction facing. It is the most versatile of all vastu yantras for existing properties with multiple defects, creating a broad-field correction energy from the primary entry point of the home.

Yantra 7: Ganesha Yantra — Obstacle Removal

Method: Place the Ganesha Yantra at the main entrance — above the door, on the entrance wall, or just inside the threshold. Ganesha is the remover of obstacles and the guardian of all new beginnings — his yantra at the entrance ensures every person who enters carries Ganesha's protective and auspicious energy into the home with them. The Ganesha yantra at the entrance is particularly recommended for homes or businesses at a new beginning — new construction, new tenancy, new business launch, or after a period of difficulty.

The science of vastu yantras is documented extensively in the Tantra Shastra, Agama Shastra, and the zone-specific chapters of the Brihat Samhita. The classical definition of a yantra is: "a geometric device that captures the cosmic energy of a deity or principle and releases it continuously into the space where it is installed." Unlike a murti (deity image) which requires daily ritual service, a properly activated yantra functions as a continuous, self-sustaining energy installation — it does not require daily worship to maintain its activation, only periodic renewal (typically on the relevant deity's day or during auspicious lunar periods).
